\subsection{Butterworth Filters}
The main objective of \deleted{this process} \added{filtering the data} \textcolor{red}{Write more accurately when you state the topic. Can you cancel all noise? Are you sure?} is to excluded all kind of noises from the EMG signal because whenever an EMG signal is being recorded from the muscle, various types of noises contaminate it. The noises are received due to electronic equipment and physiological factors. The attributes of the EMG signal depend on the internal structure of the subject, including the individual skin formation, blood flow velocity, measured skin temperatures, the tissue structure (muscle, fat, etc.), the measuring site, and more. This may have an effect on the result of feature extraction and hence affect the diagnosis of the EMG signals\cite{Chowdhury2013article}. There are two kinds of noise that I want to reduce. First, the inherent instability of the signal is caused by the firing rate of the motor units of the muscle. The firing rate of the motor units is quasi-random in nature, and it affects frequency components between 0 and 20Hz\cite{Chowdhury2013article}. Second, The electrocardiographic artifacts are due to an overlap of frequency spectra by ECG \textcolor{red}{What is ECG? You should state the long form of the abbreviation at the first occurrence.} and EMG signals. The electrical activity of the heart is the foremost interfering component for surface electromyography (sEMG) in the shoulder girdle, which is called an “electrocardiogram (ECG) artifact” \cite{Taelman2011proceedings}. Cardiac activity (ECG artifact) often contaminates EMG signals, especially in trunk muscle electromyography \cite{Willigenburg2012article}. The ECG artifact affects the frequency component at more than 100Hz\cite{Chowdhury2013article}. The Butterworth band-pass filter was used to reduce these two types of noise by combining the high and low-frequency filters. The filters will pass frequency within a range (20Hz - 100Hz) and attenuate frequencies outside the range. The band-pass filter comes from the modified low-pass filter, where the original low-pass filter can be seen in equation~\ref{eq:bandpass_filter} bellow:

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:bandpass_filter}
G(w) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 + (\frac{\Omega}{\omega_c})^{2n}}}
\end{equation}

Where $\mathnormal{w}$ is the frequency, $\mathnormal{w_c}$ is the cutoff frequency, and $\mathnormal{n}$ is the number of orders.

Chapter 3
Experiment Setup
Figure 2.1 show the big picture of the methods of this study. First, on the local
site, the Myo armband will read muscle activity as a signal that streams at 200Hz and
represents an array containing eight integer values. The data will be filtered using
a highpass filter to allow passing only high-frequency detail, attenuating the low-
frequency details. After that, the data will be cut into small sequences using sliding
windows, and to extract features from the signal, a frequency domain extractor will
be used to extract information from the signal. Based on those extracted features, I
trained GRU with a Multihead attention model to classify the signal into gestures.
